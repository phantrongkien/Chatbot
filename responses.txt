Debian:Debian (/'d?bi?n/),[5][6] also known as Debian GNU/Linux, is a free and open source[a] Linux distribution, developed by the Debian Project, which was established by Ian Murdock in August 1993. ubuntu:Ubuntu is a Linux distribution derived from Debian and composed mostly of free and open-source software. Ubuntu is officially released in multiple editions: Desktop, Server, and Core for Internet of things devices and robots. The operating system is developed by the British company Canonical and a community of other developers, under a meritocratic governance model. 
arch linux:Arch Linux (/??rt?/)[7][8] is an independently developed x86-64 general-purpose Linux distribution that strives to provide the latest stable versions of most software by following a rolling-release model.
